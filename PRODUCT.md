# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Direction (working assumption — confirm to revise)

Fresh redesign of the WordPress/Elementor site (not a faithful port), in the spirit of
the newer `Hero.astro` / `SiteLayout.astro`: bolder, spec-driven, image-led. Keeps the
committed brand identity (cyan + deep teal, Montserrat / Geist Sans). German-primary,
bilingual (DE + EN).

## Users

- **Primary — DACH leisure-boat owners.** Sailors, houseboat and day-boat owners on
  inland lakes and sheltered coast. Many German lakes ban combustion engines, so an
  emissions-free, quiet, swimmer-safe drive is not a nice-to-have — it's the price of
  access to the water. They're deciding whether to repower or buy new.
- **Secondary — B2B / OEM.** Boatbuilders, dealers and integrators evaluating the drive
  (outboard, inboard, pod) for a model line. They need specs, integration credibility,
  and a clear path to contact.
- **Tertiary — investors & press.** The partner strip (skale, bwcon, MPE Ventures,
  Zentipes) signals an early-stage, backed engineering company. They read the page for
  credibility and traction.

Context: decisions are made on the water and in a boathouse, often from a phone, often
outdoors in bright light. The page must read as clearly on a dock as at a desk.

## Product Purpose

Sylents (VERVE Water Mobility GmbH) makes the first **series-ready electric waterjet**
for broad leisure use — outboard, inboard and pod drives (1–10 kW) with a closed
impeller, internal water-cooled motor, and no external moving parts. The website's job:
prove the product is real and engineered, communicate what makes a jet better than a
propeller, and convert interest into qualified leads (contact / demo requests).

## Brand Personality

**Quiet. Precise. Engineered.** The product is literally silent and the company is an
engineering one; the brand should embody calm authority rather than shout. Calm, not
timid — confident enough to lead with real numbers and real physics. Marine, not
automotive: it belongs on a lake, not in a showroom.

## Anti-references

- **Generic "electric mobility" SaaS.** Navy/indigo gradients, the hero-metric template
  (big number, tiny label, three supporting stats), 3-card feature grids with outline
  icons. The product is hardware on water, not a dashboard.
- **Greenwashing eco-green.** Leaf icons, green-on-white "sustainability" theatre. The
  drive is emissions-free as a fact, not a moodboard. Sustainability comes from the
  engineering, not a green palette.
- **Tesla / glossy automotive showroom.** Cinematic all-black, spinning 3D product
  renders, automotive reveal theatre. Sylents is a workshop and a lake, not a stage.
- **WordPress/Elementor template density** — the very thing being migrated away from:
  cramped multi-column blocks, mismatched button styles, stock-photo chrome.

## Design Principles

1. **Engineered honesty.** Every claim is backed by a real number (power range, weight,
   runtime, "0 moving parts outside"). No metric without a source, no adjective that a
   spec couldn't replace. The spec strip is a first-class citizen, not decoration.
2. **Quiet by design.** The product's defining trait is silence; the page should feel
   calm, spaced, and unhurried. Restraint here is the brand, not a default. Motion is
   rare, deliberate, and physical (water, light, weight) — never entrance-animation reflex.
3. **Show the thing, not the brochure.** Lead with the real drive and the real water.
   Photography of the product and its environment over decorative diagrams. Where a
   diagram earns its place, it explains the jet's mechanics — it doesn't fill space.
4. **Water-native, lake-honest.** Belong to inland water: light, surface, shoreline.
   Not open-ocean drama, not pool-clean. The setting is a lake at golden hour, a boathouse,
   a hand on a tiller — physical and local (DACH), not a stock-rendered nowhere.
5. **One idea per fold.** The product is new and unfamiliar ("why a jet?"); give each
   argument a full fold and the air to land. Long, deliberate scroll over cramped grids.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** as the baseline: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast (watch the muted grays on
  tinted backgrounds and white text over photo overlays — the current heroes are at risk),
  ≥3:1 large text, keyboard-reachable nav and forms, labelled inputs.
- **Reduced motion** respected throughout (the partner carousel and any scroll/entrance
  motion need a static alternative).
- **Outdoor readability**: decisions happen on a dock in bright light — favor higher
  contrast and larger tap targets than a desk-only design would settle for.
- **Bilingual parity**: German and English must reach the same quality and completeness,
  not EN-as-afterthought.
